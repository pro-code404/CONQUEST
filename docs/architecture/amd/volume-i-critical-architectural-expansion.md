### Authority clause

This volume defines **foundational architectural expansion** for Conquest — cognitive hierarchy, orchestration, agents, decision engine, memory graph, execution, Command Center, and completeness law.

No subsystem may be designed or implemented unless it conforms to this volume and CCIS.

This volume does **not** define databases, schemas, APIs, storage technology, or user interfaces.

### Reconciliation note

Where this volume's §19.0 execution lifecycle or hierarchy ordering differs from CCIS §II or [ADR-0007](../adr/0007-ccis-cognitive-lifecycle-order.md), **CCIS and ADR-0007 prevail**. AMD IV §69 and SDD-II Part 5 express engineering reconciliation.

## 11.0 COGNITIVE HIERARCHY ARCHITECTURE

Conquest shall operate through a structured intelligence hierarchy rather than a single reasoning process.

Every request entering the system shall pass through specialized intelligence layers.

The intelligence hierarchy shall consist of:

User Input

↓

Orchestration Layer

↓

Understanding Intelligence

↓

Context Intelligence

↓

Memory Intelligence

↓

Research Intelligence

↓

Reasoning Intelligence

↓

Prediction Intelligence

↓

Decision Intelligence

↓

Verification Intelligence

↓

Execution Intelligence

↓

Learning Intelligence

↓

Feedback Intelligence

Each intelligence layer possesses a defined responsibility.

No intelligence layer shall assume responsibilities belonging to another layer.

The objective of the hierarchy is specialization, accountability, explainability, and optimization.

## 12.0 CENTRAL ORCHESTRATION ARCHITECTURE

The Orchestrator is the highest operational authority inside Conquest.

The Orchestrator does not perform intelligence work.

The Orchestrator coordinates intelligence work.

Responsibilities include:

• Request Classification

• Intent Identification

• Priority Assignment

• Risk Assessment

• Agent Selection

• Memory Selection

• Research Routing

• Validation Requirements

• Execution Routing

• Escalation Decisions

• Resource Allocation

• Workflow Construction

Every subsystem reports to the Orchestrator.

No subsystem may bypass orchestration.

## 13.0 AGENT COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORK

Conquest shall function as a coordinated ecosystem of specialist intelligence agents.

Agents communicate through structured intelligence packets.

Each packet shall contain:

Source Agent

Destination Agent

Task ID

Evidence

Confidence Score

Reasoning Summary

Validation Status

Timestamp

Agents shall never exchange unrestricted conversational responses.

Agent communication must remain structured, auditable, and explainable.

## 15.0 DECISION ENGINE ARCHITECTURE

Every major recommendation must pass through the Decision Engine.

The Decision Engine evaluates:

Evidence Score

Confidence Score

Risk Score

Impact Score

Probability Score

Cost Score

Time Score

Complexity Score

Dependency Score

Opportunity Score

The Decision Engine shall rank available recommendations before approval.

Recommendations shall not be selected solely because they were generated first.

The strongest recommendation is the recommendation with the strongest supporting evidence.

## 20.0 COMMAND CENTER ARCHITECTURE

The Command Center shall function as the primary intelligence workspace.

The purpose of the Command Center is not visualization.

Performance Monitoring

Learning Insights

Strategic Recommendations
